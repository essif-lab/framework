Short Description​

A capability (of a ) is the (named) combination of

  1. the means of a specific to get something done - in other words the means to ensure that certain results are realized (typically associated with an of that );
  2. the process that ensures that its (business) for getting that something done are being created and maintained, and
  3. the process that creates and maintains the (operational) , such that every that has a task in getting this something done can find and interpret a and use it as it executes in such tasks..

The name of a capability typically refers to a (set of) function(s) that stakeholders (e.g. architects, domain experts, business people, developers) can easily relate to. In contexts, names such as 'issuer capability', 'revocation capability' might appear.

The description of a capability (of a ) should specify

  1. its name;
  2. its scope, i.e.
  • the functions that define the 'getting something done';
  • the (other) that are expected to be in place, and that the relies on to function as specified;

  • the (e.g. service providers) that (operationally) supply the that the itself relies on;
  • that are tasked with the creation and operational maintenance of the means that the needs to dispose of (and that may rely on other ) in order to have this capability.
  1. the that this has towards that are tasked with the operational execution of (some of) these functions1.
